# Temple of Evil Chaos

The chaotic evil religious organization centered in the Shrine of Evil Chaos (Lair K of the [[Wiki/Locations/Caves-of-Chaos]]). Maintains an active spy inside [[Wiki/Locations/Keep-on-the-Borderlands]]. The primary antagonist faction of [[Wiki/Modules/B2-Keep-on-the-Borderlands]].

## Overview

The Temple worships a demon-associated chaos deity. Its physical presence is the Shrine (Lair K) — a multi-chamber complex of worked stone with undead guards, evil clerics, and a resonant evil aura. Strategically, the faction seeks to prevent adventurers from clearing the Caves by sponsoring humanoid tribes as buffer forces and placing spies in the Keep.

The four cursed bronze relics in the chapel (K-55) are the Temple's most dangerous trap: characters who fail to save become permanent servants of Chaos.

## Leadership

- [[Wiki/NPCs/Shrine-Evil-Cleric]] — Shrine master (C3, room K-59); commands all undead and clerical forces
- [[Wiki/NPCs/Evil-Priest-Spy]] — Field agent embedded in the Keep (area 7b); handles intelligence and party sabotage

## Goals

**Stated goals:** None presented to the outside world.

**True goals (gm_only):** Maintain the humanoid lairs as buffer forces against Keep adventurers. Corrupt and destroy adventuring parties before they threaten the Shrine. Expand the Temple's influence and conduct dark rites to their demon patron. The spy priest's secondary goal is active subversion and recruitment — using bribes and manipulation to turn Keep inhabitants toward chaos.

## Party Relationship

Unknown to party.

## Conspiracy Connections

- **Intelligence network:** [[Wiki/NPCs/Evil-Priest-Spy]] monitors the entry scribe's records in the Keep (area 3) to track adventuring party movements, then relays this information to the Shrine
- **Buffer force:** The humanoid tribes of the Caves (Kobolds, Orcs, Goblins, Hobgoblins, Bugbears, Gnolls) are not formal members of the Temple but serve as de facto shock troops; the Shrine benefits from their presence without directly controlling them
- **Undead army:** The Shrine commands dozens of undead warriors (see GM Notes below), making it the most militarily concentrated threat in the Caves

## GM Notes

*gm_only*

If the spy priest is exposed or killed, the Temple loses its entire intelligence network and advance warning system.

**The cursed relics (room K-55):** Four ancient bronze vessels — shallow bowl, 2 goblets, ewer. Each character who picks one up must save vs. Magic at −2. Success: a feeling of great evil, character may voluntarily drop it. Failure: character falls under demonic compulsion within 6 days, returns to the shrine, and becomes a permanent guard. Reversal: dispel magic + bless has a 60% chance on day 1, decreasing 10%/day through day 6. Detect evil reveals ugly purple glow; good characters feel instant loathing.

**The great bell (room K-58):** Sounding it deploys undead warriors from room K-57 on the same round (skeletons issue south, zombies go north to line the temple walls). Three voices chanting simultaneously ring the bell automatically by magic. Even one chanting voice alerts the guards.

**Full undead command structure:**
- 8 wandering zombie guards (summoned by noise, arrive in 3 rounds)
- 8 zombies in guard room K-53 (amulets: turned as ghouls)
- 12 dormant skeletons in K-52 (activate on dais touch; amulets: turned as zombies)
- 20 skeletons + 20 zombies in hall K-57 (deploy on bell; all have amulets)
- 3 plate-mailed zombies in anteroom K-59g (AC 2, hp 13 each; turned as ghouls)

**Amulet of protection from turning:** All undead guard groups and clerics wear these. Effect: clerics attempt to turn them as if one category higher (zombies turned as ghouls, skeletons turned as zombies, etc.). Proper robes (red/black for acolytes, black/maroon for adepts) plus a matching amulet allow passage without attack — unless the priest commands the undead to attack anyway.
