# Gnoll Warband J

A gnoll warband occupying Lair J in [[Wiki/Locations/Caves-of-Chaos]]. Larger than individual lair groups but not as organized as the hobgoblins. Has a loose defensive alliance with the orc tribes — gnoll and orc survivors reinforce each other's areas if either is pressed.

## Overview

The gnoll chieftain's quarters contain a curious secret: a human thief's skeleton with a broken leg lies beside a secret door, apparently having died trying to escape. The thief's purse holds 12 gems (50 gp each) and the skeleton still wears intact elven boots. The secret door connects to the Shrine's storage chamber (K-63), making Gnoll Lair J an unexpected back entrance to the [[Wiki/Factions/Temple-of-Evil-Chaos]] complex.

**Approximate strength:**
- 9 male fighters + 16 females + 18 young (non-combatant)
- Chieftain + 2 sons + 4 female bodyguards
- Good armory (Room 48): 7 shields, dwarf-sized chain mail, 12 hand axes, 3 longbows, 5 quivers

## Leadership

- **Gnoll Chieftain** — Room 50. AC 3 (pieces of plate mail), HD 3, hp 17, D 4-10 (2d4+2, Strength). Silver armbands (50 gp each), 39 gp in belt pouch. Metal pot under flagstone in fireplace alcove hides 200 cp + 157 sp + 76 ep + 139 gp. Can escape up the fireplace chimney if pressed.
- **Two Sons** — HD 2+1, hp 10 each, D 3-9 (2d4+1).

## Goals

Survival and raiding. Loose cooperative arrangement with orc survivors. The chieftain and Orc C leader know each other; survivors migrate between lairs.

## Party Relationship

Not yet encountered.

## Conspiracy Connections

No direct link to the [[Wiki/Factions/Temple-of-Evil-Chaos]], but the secret door from Room 50 to Shrine Room K-63 means the gnolls sit on a back passage to the Shrine that neither side is likely aware of (the thief found it and apparently died for the knowledge).

## GM Notes

*gm_only*

**Key intelligence value of Room 50:**
- The dead thief with elven boots is an in-world mystery hook — who was he, why was he there, where was he going?
- The secret door to K-63 (Shrine storage) is unknown to the gnolls. It is a potential back route into the Shrine that bypasses the entire undead-guarded approach.
- Gelatinous cube in K-63 is an obstacle guarding the wand of enemy detection (9 charges).

**Ale trap (Room 48):** Exceptional ale barrel — 5-in-6 chance per taste that character spends 1-4 turns drinking, then suffers -2 to hit for as many turns. Causes singing and foolish behavior. Easy to use against careless parties.

**Cursed sword:** Room 48 also contains a sword -1, cursed. Players who loot carelessly may take it without examination.

Alliance: if gnolls are losing, surviving gnolls reinforce orc areas and vice versa. Factor this into encounter counts if both lairs have been hit.
