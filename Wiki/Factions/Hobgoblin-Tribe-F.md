# Hobgoblin Tribe F

The most organized and militarily disciplined humanoid faction in [[Wiki/Locations/Caves-of-Chaos]]. Occupies Lair F, entered through a barred oaken door decorated with skulls and a warning sign in common runes. Holds six prisoners in their torture chamber.

## Overview

Unlike the kobolds, goblins, or gnolls, the hobgoblin tribe maintains standing guards, a proper armory, organized alarm procedures, and coordinated room coverage. Their chief is one of the toughest leaders in the Caves (AC 2, HD 5, hp 22). They exploit the adjacent goblin tribe (Lair D) by stealing food through a secret door. They have a mercenary relationship with [[Wiki/NPCs/The-Minotaur]] through the bugbear clan.

**Approximate strength:**
- ~14 males (fighting) + females + young
- Chief + 4 large female bodyguards (fight as males)
- Armory contains significant stores (plate mail, chain mail, elf-sized chain, 13 crossbows, 220+ arrows, 51 spears, 19 pole arms)

## Leadership

- **Hobgoblin Chief** — Area 30. AC 2 (plate mail, shield), HD 5, hp 22, D 3-10 (Strength + skill); carries silver + gem belt (600 gp). Hidden false bottom in iron box contains 25 pp + 200 gp + 115 ep + 400 sp + 100 gp gem + potion of poison. Wand of paralyzation (7 charges) hidden in kindling near fireplace.
- **Torture Chamber Guards** — Area 24. Two large hobgoblins; one carries a whip (15' reach, stuns victim 1-2 rounds on hit).

## Goals

Secure resources and prey. Exploit goblin neighbors. Maintain defensible lair with prisoner stock for feast and leverage.

## Party Relationship

Not yet encountered.

## Conspiracy Connections

The hobgoblins have no direct relationship with [[Wiki/Factions/Temple-of-Evil-Chaos]], but their prisoners and armory make them a significant independent power in the Caves. If defeated, survivors reinforce the goblin tribe (Lair D).

## GM Notes

*gm_only*

**Prisoner rescue hooks (Room 24):**
- **Merchant (prisoner #1):** Half-dead, scheduled to be eaten tonight. Rescue = 100 gp reward from Guild + honorary Guild status + 1-year fee exemption.
- **Merchant's wife (prisoner #4):** Has a dagger +1 in her room back at the Keep — she personally gives it to rescuers.
- **Two men-at-arms (prisoners #3, #6):** Will serve rescuers for 1 year for room and board if given arms and armor.
- **Orc (prisoner #2):** Will fight hobgoblins, then flee and inform Lair B.
- **Crazy gnoll (prisoner #5):** Will attack rescuers if freed. Weakened (1-6 damage only).

Keys to prisoner chains hang in the SW corner of Room 24. Keys to Room 37 (Spoils Room) are in the chief's pouch (with 29 pp + 3 gems at 50 gp each).

**Wand of paralyzation** (7 charges) hidden in kindling by the fireplace in Room 30 — only found on careful search. This is the most valuable tactical item in Lair F.

If the chief is killed, survivors will flee unless opponents are clearly weak. 2 bugbears are out hunting and return the day after the party first enters Lair H — they can be placed on guard duty at Room 35.

## Combat Behavior

*gm_only*

Militaristic and disciplined. Fight tactically — retreat in good order, call for reinforcements, fall back to defensible positions when losing. They do not stand and die out of pride; they yield ground to regroup. The only faction in the Caves that treats combat as a military problem rather than a brawl.

On a morale failure, hobgoblins execute an organized withdrawal. The party should not assume a retreating hobgoblin is a defeated one — he may be repositioning crossbowmen.
