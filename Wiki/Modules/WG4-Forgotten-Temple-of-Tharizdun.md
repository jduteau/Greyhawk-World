# WG4 — The Forgotten Temple of Tharizdun

A wilderness dungeon discovered between the A-series and G-series. Not part of the Lolth conspiracy directly — a deliberate detour that recontextualises it. Tharizdun is a vastly more ancient evil. Lolth actively avoids him, which makes her more formidable: she is a power even the Elder Eye cannot command.

## Campaign Role

A tonal pivot. The party has been tracking a coherent (if vast) conspiracy. Tharizdun is not part of it — and that absence is significant. This module delivers the first moment the party senses that the layers of evil they have been fighting are themselves aware of, and frightened by, something older.

It also delivers Lolth's name in a context entirely separate from the conspiracy, written by people who died millennia ago.

**Atmosphere:** The temple has been sealed for centuries. As the party approaches, magical sensitivity and strong faith both register something vast and wrong — not through any mechanical ability, but through atmosphere and character.
