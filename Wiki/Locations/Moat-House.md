# The Moat House

A ruined fortified warpost of the Temple of Elemental Evil, located 2–3 miles from [[Wiki/Locations/Village-of-Hommlet]] along an overgrown, seldom-used track (area 33 on the village map). The primary dungeon location for [[Wiki/Modules/T1-Village-of-Hommlet]].

## Overview

The Moat House served as an advance warning post and staging area during the Temple of Elemental Evil's campaign of conquest. After the Temple's defeat it was abandoned and fell into ruin. The surrounding moat still holds water and is home to dangerous creatures. The structure has two levels: a ruined ground-level fortress and a dungeon level beneath.

The Moat House is now **reoccupied** — bandits and monsters have moved back in, directed by agents of the Temple who are using it as a forward base while the Temple reconstitutes itself.

## Geography

**Access:** Overgrown track east of Hommlet, ~2–3 miles. The track is passable but obviously long-disused. Locals know it exists but have little reason to use it.

**The Moat:** Surrounds the structure. Still holds water. Contains giant frogs and other hazards. Drawbridge/gatehouse controls main access.

**Ground Level (ruined):**
- Gatehouse and towers — partially intact; current bandit garrison
- Courtyard — overgrown, partially collapsed
- Great hall and outbuildings — heavily ruined, dangerous footing
- Towers — upper floors collapsed; lower sections usable as guard posts

**Dungeon Level (below):**
- Accessed via stairs inside the main structure
- Better preserved than the surface
- Contains the true leadership of the current occupation

## Current Status

Not yet visited by the party. The Moat House is actively occupied by a bandit company and associated monsters. The dungeon level conceals a Temple of Elemental Evil operative directing operations.

## Known Inhabitants

*gm_only*

- **Lareth the Beautiful** — CE cleric of Lolth, Temple of Elemental Evil agent; commands the dungeon level; his true allegiance is unknown to the bandits above; strikingly handsome, utterly ruthless; key intelligence link to the Temple's reconstitution effort
- **Bandit company** — garrisoned in the gatehouse and ground level towers; armed fighters loyal to coin, not cause
- **Giant frogs** — in the moat; aggressive
- **Undead** — scattered through the ruined upper works; remnants of the original Temple garrison

## What the Village Knows

Common knowledge in Hommlet:
- The old Moat House is out there on the overgrown track east of town
- It was a Temple of Elemental Evil outpost during the war
- It has been abandoned for years
- Dangerous to enter — the place is a ruin and the moat has monsters

What some locals suspect but don't advertise:
- There may be activity out there again (travelers on the road have reported lights, sounds)
- Elmo ([[Wiki/NPCs/Elmo]]) is actively monitoring this; he has told the Viscount's office

What Gundigoot ([[Wiki/NPCs/Gundigoot]]) will tell adventurers:
- Confirms the location (east track, 2–3 miles)
- Identifies it as a former Temple warpost without alarm — it's common history
- Warns that the moat is dangerous and the structure is unstable
- Will note that a current inn guest (Spugnoir, upper room 5) has also been asking about the place — he doesn't say this out of concern, but as a conversational tidbit; he judges adventurers' character while sharing it

## Party History

No visits recorded.

## GM Notes

*gm_only*

- Lareth the Beautiful is the sleeper in this module — party members of either gender may find him genuinely attractive, which the module uses as a disarming hook. He will not hesitate to destroy them.
- The bandit garrison does not know Lareth's true affiliation. They believe he is a mercenary commander with good organizational skills and access to Temple-era maps.
- The Moat House connects upward to the Temple of Elemental Evil (T2) — clearing it is the narrative bridge between T1 and T2.
- Spugnoir (inn guest) is after scrolls only; he will shadow a party if not invited. He has no agenda regarding Lareth and will flee if combat gets serious.
- Elmo knows more than he lets on about current activity at the Moat House. If the party develops trust with him, he can provide useful intelligence.
- A pass scarab "TZGY" at [[Wiki/NPCs/Rannos-Davi]]'s traders' shop provides access to Temple areas — the traders are more dangerous than the Moat House.
