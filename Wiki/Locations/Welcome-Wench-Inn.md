# Inn of the Welcome Wench

The primary social hub of [[Wiki/Locations/Village-of-Hommlet]], renowned for excellent food and drink across the region. Area 7 in the village. Three floors plus cellar, run by Ostler Gundigoot (F2, militia sergeant).

## Overview

The best inn for many miles in any direction. Passing merchants make a point of stopping. Always 4–16 customers in the common room (double in evenings). Food includes venison, salmon, roast goose, stuffed pheasant, steak and kidney pie with mushrooms, and boiled crayfish. Drinks span local ales and beers to wines, meads, and brandies from across the Flanaess.

Gundigoot is sharp-eyed, judges character well, and talks freely while saying little. He will serve any non-troublemaker. The first potboy is an aspiring druid who serves as courier to [[Wiki/NPCs/Jaroo-Ashstaff]], relaying news about interesting visitors.

## Geography

**First Floor:**
- **Room 1 — Common Room:** 4–16 customers; rough-hewn tables, tree-trunk pillars dark with smoke; barmaids and potboys circulate
- **Room 2 — Private Dining Room:** For visiting nobles and rich merchants; tapestries and paintings
- **Room 3 — Private Meeting Room:** Dark corner, inconspicuous; secret door (press-and-slide panel upward) leads to narrow stone stairs down to cellar secret room (8)
- **Room 4 — Bar:** Gundigoot's station; cash box holds 11 pp, 47 gp, 17 ep, 33 sp, 61 cp (taken upstairs nightly); ~100 gp equivalent in his apron pockets
- **Room 5 — Kitchen:** Huge fireplace; Goodwife Gundigoot in charge; stairs to cellar and up to owner's apartment

**Upper Floor (guest rooms):**
- **Room 1 — Zert's Room:** Chaotic evil fighter (F2, hp 12, scale mail + shield + sword + dagger); Temple of Elemental Evil spy waiting for "caravan from south"; immune to heavy drinking (200% capacity); locked coffer hidden under clothing: 100 100-gp gems, 100 pp, 265 gp; medium warhorse + lance in stable
- **Room 2 — Noble Suite:** 5 gp/night with breakfast; sitting room + inner chamber with featherbed
- **Rooms 3–4 & 6–7:** Standard private rooms (1–2.5 gp/night)
- **Room 5 — Spugnoir's Room:** Chaotic neutral M-U (level 2, hp 4, knows detect magic / read magic / sleep only); traveled in on merchant wagon; plans to quietly search the Moat House for spells; will join a party if offered all scrolls found; otherwise will shadow the group
- **Room 8 — Furnok of Ferd:** 4th level thief (hp 18, ring of invisibility, scroll of protection from magic, short sword, +1 dagger in boot); gambler with loaded dice (75% to win at dice, 60% at cards); will join party for equal share + anything he can surreptitiously lift; will offer his scroll as good faith deposit
- **Room 9 — Kobort and Turuko:** Kobort (F2, hp 20, S 18/10, splint mail, sword + dagger; lance, flail, axe, and heavy warhorse in stable); Turuko (Monk 3, hp 11, Bakluni, lawful evil, quarterstaff + 2 daggers); plan to accompany a small party, hang back in fights, then slay the weakened survivors and rob them
- **Room 10 — Dormitory:** 1 sp/night; 2–12 sleepers on pallets; free hot tea and fresh bread in morning
- **Room 11 — Spare Room / Potboy Quarters**
- **Rooms 12–15 — Staff and Owner Quarters:** Gundigoot's private apartment includes a secret compartment in the north wall with a locked iron box: 6 pieces jewelry (1,000 ×2, 2,000, 3,000, 4,000, 5,000 gp), sack of 100 pp, sack of 400 gp; concealed attic access allowing ceiling-panel surveillance of guest rooms

**Cellar:**
- **Rooms 1–5:** Storage, summer kitchen, locked cool storage for perishables, wine cellar, main storage
- **Room 6 — Huge Casks:** Three great casks; one empty cask has a section that swings up for entry; a hidden catch opens the far end; a dagger blade in the proper crack opens a secret door in the fieldstone wall leading to room 8
- **Room 7 — Ashpit:** Stone-walled; dead end deliberately included to frustrate searchers
- **Room 8 — Disused Secret Room:** Former resistance HQ during Temple of Elemental Evil war; nearly soundproof (false basement crawl space between ceiling and inn floor); bunks along walls; stored arms: 12 long swords, 12 short swords, 12 daggers, 3 maces, 7 battleaxes, 3 hand axes, 20 javelins, 8 spears, 7 crossbows, 130 bolts, pole arms, plus bucklers, shields, helmets, 12 each leather jacks

## GM Notes

*gm_only*

- Zert (upper room 1) does NOT know that Rannos Davi and Gremag at the traders are also Temple agents. The traders know who Zert is. If the party befriends Zert and the traders simultaneously, neither will volunteer the connection.
- Gundigoot's ceiling surveillance system (from loft via attic, sliding panels) allows quiet observation of any guest room. He will use it on highly suspicious guests and will alert Jaroo if needed.
- Furnok's scroll of protection from magic is a legitimate good-faith offer — it's actually valuable. His "equal share" deal is not deceptive; his side theft is the hidden catch.
- Kobort & Turuko's plan requires the party to be weakened. They will join a small group, be cowardly in fights, then strike when the party is reduced. Against a large or obviously powerful group they simply spy and wait for an opportunity.
- The cellar secret room (8) represents the village's prepared defenses against another Temple resurgence. Gundigoot knows about it.
- Spugnoir will shadow a party that doesn't include him, hoping to benefit from their dungeon-clearing. If he finds scrolls in the Moat House he will not report back.
