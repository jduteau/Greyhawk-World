# Caves of Chaos

A ravine complex containing seven distinct humanoid lairs and a shrine to evil chaos. The primary adventuring site in [[Wiki/Modules/B2-Keep-on-the-Borderlands]], located in the wilderness near [[Wiki/Locations/Keep-on-the-Borderlands]].

## Overview

The Caves of Chaos fill a ravine of worn, cave-pocked rock. Each lair is semi-independent but loosely connected through alliances, rivalries, and the overarching influence of the [[Wiki/Factions/Temple-of-Evil-Chaos]] (Lair K). Monster losses in all lairs cannot be replaced during normal play — the Caves grow safer with each successful raid.

## Geography

The caves are lettered A through K, plus the Shunned Cavern (G) and the Minotaur's Labyrinth (I).

---

**Lair A — Kobold Tribe**

2-in-6 chance of ambush outside the cave mouth (8 kobolds from the trees). Pit trap 30' inside (3-in-6 chance front rank falls; 10' deep, 1–6 damage; planks at Room 1).

- **Room 1 — Guard Room:** 6 kobold guards; spear throwers; one flees to warn areas 4 and 6
- **Room 2 — Giant Rats:** 18 giant rats (disease risk on bite); rat leader wears thin silver chain + 5 gems (400 gp total)
- **Room 3 — Food Storage:** Locked; dried meat, grain, vegetables; nothing of value
- **Room 4 — Guard Room:** 3 large kobolds in chain mail; fire down passage at −2 to hit; 2d6 gp each
- **Room 5 — Kobold Chieftain's Room:** Chieftain (HD 2, hp 8, battle axe); key to Room 3; large gem on golden chain (1,200 gp); 5 females; hidden 50 gp sewn in blanket; locked chest (203 cp + 61 sp + 22 ep)
- **Room 6 — Common Chamber:** 17 males + 23 females + 8 young; silk piece worth 150 gp in litter

---

**Lair B — Orc Tribe B**

Entry decorated with skulls. Watcher (area g.) disguised as a trophy — ducks and sends goblin head substitute, then alerts Room 7.

- **Rooms 7–8 — Guard Rooms:** 4 orcs each; spears for hurling; d8 ep each
- **Room 9 — Banquet Area:** Empty; large fireplace; orc leader holds court here
- **Room 10 — Common Room:** 12 male orcs + 18 females + 9 young; 2d6 sp per male
- **Room 11 — Storage:** Locked; 3 shields, 17 spears, 2 battle axes, 1 crossbow + 60 bolts
- **Room 12 — Orc Leader's Room:** Chain mail + shield+1 + mace; 4 HD, hp 15, +2 damage; 31 gp + ring with gem (700 gp); tapestry covers secret door to Lair C; two mates fight as males
- **Area i — Alcove:** 2 chain mail suits, 4 swords, locked iron chest (205 cp + 286 sp + 81 gp + 13 pp); niche hides potion of healing + scroll with 6-die fireball spell

*If leader slain: all orcs flee to Lair C, taking all valuables; Lair B deserted thereafter.*

---

**Lair C — Orc Tribe C**

Trip-wire alarm net at entrance — camouflaged, 10'×18', tarred rope; traps victims 1–4 rounds; Room 14 orcs arrive in 1 round. Net spotted on 1-in-6 per careful observer.

- **Room 13 — Forgotten Room:** Secret meeting room shared by leaders of B and C; locked chest (bow, quiver 20 arrows, 2 swords, 2 daggers); 2 shields on south wall; under old bucket: 2 pouches (each: 1 gem 50 gp + 10 gp + 20 sp), guarded by 2 giant centipedes
- **Room 14 — Common Chamber:** 9 male orcs + 8 females + 3 young; d20 sp per male
- **Room 15 — Common Hall:** 6 males (2 with crossbows) + 4 females; orcs arrive at entrance in 3 rounds after net alarm
- **Room 16 — Leader's Room:** Guard always posted (cannot be surprised); barrels/boxes supply cache; small wine barrel (worth 55 gp)
- **Leader's quarters (east):** Plate mail + shield + sword; hand axe +1 (hurls and still attacks normally); silver belt + gold buckle (160 gp); sword with 100 gp gem in pommel; 8 gp + 17 ep + 5 sp; mate has ivory bracelet (100 gp); small chest contains 50 pp tied with rope of climbing; copper bowl (looks like 10 sp, actually 50 gp)

---

**Lair D — Goblin Tribe**

Very busy — 1-in-6 chance per 10' of wandering encounter; check every 30' (3-in-6 cumulative). Wandering goblins carry d6 bags of good foodstuffs. When encountered, they attack and shout "Bree-Yark!" (alarm).

- **Room 17 — Guard Chamber:** 6 goblin guards; barrel with 60 spears; d40 cp + d4 sp each
- **Room 18 — Guard Chamber:** 6 guards; watches east; sack of 250 gp for ogre bribe hidden in water barrel by secret door to Lair E
- **Room 19 — Common Room:** 10 males + 14 females + 6 young; d6 sp per male, 2d6 cp per female
- **Room 20 — Chieftain's Room:** Goblin chief (AC 4, HD 3, hp 11, D 2–7); 3 guards; 18 gp + 2 pp in purse; silver cup (90 gp) under bed; secret drawer under bench contains treasure tapestry (900 gp gold/silver threads); pewter bowl holds 273 sp + 321 cp
- **Room 21 — Storage:** 4 guards with ready crossbows at position g.; if party stays 1+ turn, 4 hobgoblins come through secret door from Lair F

*If soundly defeated: goblins flee east to hobgoblins (area 17 → area 23) and join forces.*

---

**Lair E — Ogre Cave**

Huge ogre (AC 4, HD 4+1, hp 25, D 3–12) uses bear skin as bed. Mercenary: serves goblins (paid 250 gp from Room 18 if tossed). Bribable — if offered more than goblins paid, 90% chance he takes both payments and returns to lair. Offer 20+ gp: 90% chance lets party pass; will attack if he sees them again.

- **Room 22:** Ogre sits on leather bag containing 7 sacks: 287 sp; hard cheese; 182 cp + 91 ep; 289 gp; keg of brandy (80 gp); 303 cp; 241 lead-gold coins (1 cp each, only looks like gold)
- **Under bones (south cave):** 6 magic arrows +1, potion of invisibility, scroll (cure light wounds + hold person — cleric spells)

---

**Lair F — Hobgoblin Tribe**

Barred oaken door; skulls + ominous warning in common runes. Secret mechanism to unbar from outside (1-in-6 per person to detect). Forced: three 1s on d6; noise alerts area 26. Knock spell: bar falls noisily, 2 rounds before guards react.

- **Room 23 — Common Room:** 5 males + 8 females + 3 young; males watch the east door to Goblin Lair
- **Room 24 — Torture Chamber:** 2 large hobgoblins (one with whip: hits knock victim prone + stunned 1–2 rounds); **6 prisoners chained to walls:** (1) half-dead merchant — Guild pays 100 gp + honorary status + 1-year fee exemption if rescued; (2) orc who will fight hobgoblins then flee; (3) man-at-arms (F1) — will serve rescuers 1 year for room/board if given gear; (4) merchant's wife — has dagger +1 in her Keep room; (5) crazy gnoll — will attack rescuers; (6) another man-at-arms as #3; keys hang in southwest corner
- **Room 25 — Common Chamber:** 4 males + 5 females + 9 young; preparing for feast; pewter dishes (25 gp set)
- **Room 26 — Guard Room:** 6 hobgoblins (3 with crossbows); rush to entry if door battered; d6 each of gp/sp/cp
- **Room 27 — Armory:** 3 guards in chain mail; secret entrance to area 31; substantial armor/weapon stores (plate mail, chain mail, elf-sized chain, 13 crossbows, 51 spears, 19 pole arms, 220+ arrows with 14 silver-headed, 9 score bolts)
- **Room 28 — Storeroom:** 1 guard; goods looted from goblins
- **Room 29 — Guard Room:** 2 guards + 2 females; females alert areas 30 and 31 if intruders spotted
- **Room 30 — Chief's Quarters:** Chief (AC 2, HD 5, hp 22, D 3–10, ML 10); 4 large females; false bottom in iron box: 25 pp + 200 gp + 115 ep + 400 sp + 100 gp gem + potion of poison; wand of paralyzation (7 charges) hidden in kindling near fireplace; silver + gem belt (600 gp)
- **Room 31 — Guard Room:** 4 guards; alerts adjacent areas

---

**Lair G — Shunned Cavern**

Even the ogre avoids this place. Horrible stench. Even normal inhabitants stay away.

- **Room 32 — Empty Gallery:** Bones and rotting corpses; 1 coin per round searching (1–2 = cp, 3–4 = sp, 5–6 = ep); searching noise may attract monsters (roll: 1=owlbear, 2=2d12 giant rats, 3=gray ooze, 4–6=nothing)
- **Room 33 — Shallow Pool:** Wet walls and floor; blind white fish; jewel-encrusted goblet (1,300 gp) in the water; 3 gray ooze (AC 6, HD 3★, hp 15 each; 1-in-20 chance to notice unless probing or fully lit ceiling); first round 1–8 damage + destroys foot/leg protection; thereafter 2–16 per attack
- **Room 34 — Owlbear Den:** Owlbear (AC 5, HD 5, hp 30, 3 attacks D 1–8 each); sleeping after eating a gnoll; no treasure; protection from undead scroll in bone tube (1-in-6 per searcher per round to find)

---

**Lair H — Bugbear Clan**

Deceptive welcoming signs in multiple languages. Guards offer food-on-skewers, then use them as clubs with +2 surprise bonus.

- **Room 35 — Guard Room:** 3 bugbears (HD 3+1, hp 11 each, D 2–8); 2d10 gp each; large gong for alarm
- **Room 36 — Chieftain's Room:** Chief (HD 4+1, hp 18, D 3–12); hand axe +1 on wall (hurls first); female mate (HD 3+1, hp 12, gold earrings 100 gp); 6 silk pieces in bedding (20 gp each); gray chest on ceiling ledge: 1,462 sp + ivory/alabaster statue (200 gp) + 2 potions of healing (break if roughly handled); key to Room 37 in chief's pouch with 29 pp + 3 gems (50 gp each); knows secret door to Lair I (escape route)
- **Room 37 — Spoils Room:** Locked (chief's key); shield +1 (used as herb tray); quality foodstuffs + leather hides + 3 barrels ale + tun wine + small keg oil (400 gp total if sold at Keep, excluding shield and oil)
- **Room 38 — Common Room:** 3 males + 7 females + 3 young; silver urn (175 gp) blackened near fireplace
- **Room 39 — Guard Room:** 2 males + 3 females; tend slaves; keys to Rooms 40 and 41 on opposite wall
- **Room 40 — Slave Pen:** 3 kobolds, 1 goblin, 4 orcs, 2 humans (optionally + 1 dwarf + 2 elves); chained; all fight bugbears if armed; humans behave as Hobgoblin prisoners; dwarf/elves may help while party remains in Caves
- **Room 41 — Slave Pen:** 3 hobgoblins, 2 gnolls, 1 rebel bugbear (will fight all other inhabitants), 1 hero (F4, Str 18, hp 24, evil — will betray or rob rescuers when opportunity arises); chained

*2 bugbears out hunting return day after first party visit with human corpse + 83 gp.*

---

**Lair I — Minotaur's Labyrinth**

Magical direction confusion begins 30' inside (special spell — DM misdirects: call "southeast" for northeast, "east" for west, etc.; same passage may be named differently on return). Party can attempt save each turn on 19–20 to throw off effect.

Minotaur (AC 4, HD 6, hp 35, ML 12) is mercenary for bugbears (1 human slave per 3 days of service). Only escape: secret door to Bugbear Room 36 (slab, 3 humans to move; searcher finds grip on roll of 1 on d6) or flee out and climb a tree.

- **Room 42 — Stirge Cave:** 13 stirges (90% heard hooting; attach and drain 1–4 hp/round after first hit); hungry, no treasure
- **Rooms 43–44 — Fire Beetles:** 3 in room 43, 2 in room 44; glands glow red 10' radius for 1–6 days after death
- **Room 45 — The Minotaur:** Behind secret stone slab: locked chest with poison needle (930 gp + 310 ep), staff of healing, plate mail +1 (man or elf sized), locked coffer (potions: gaseous form + healing + growth), locked chest (3 pieces jewelry: 1,600 + 900 + 600 gp)

---

**Lair J — Gnoll Warband**

Loose alliance with orcs — survivors may reinforce orc areas and vice versa.

- **Room 46 — Guard Room:** 4 gnolls always on duty; 2 with bows (fire then flee for help while 2 melee); d8 each ep/sp/cp
- **Room 47 — Guard Room:** 3 males + 5 females; sable cloak (450 gp) on wall among hides
- **Room 48 — Locked Room:** Armory; 7 shields, dwarf-sized chain, 12 hand axes, 3 longbows, 5 quivers; sword −1 cursed; ale barrel (exceptional — 5-in-6 chance each taste causes 1–4 turns of drunken behavior, −2 to hit)
- **Room 49 — Common Room:** 6 males + 11 females + 18 young; d6 ep + d6 sp per male, d10 sp per female
- **Room 50 — Chieftain's Quarters:** Chief (AC 3, HD 3, hp 17, D 4–10); 2 sons (HD 2+1, hp 10 each); 4 females; silver armbands (50 gp each) on chief; 39 gp in belt pouch; sons have d10 each gp/ep/sp; each female has silver neck chain (30 gp) + 2d6 ep; metal pot under flagstone in fireplace alcove: 200 cp + 157 sp + 76 ep + 139 gp; thief skeleton at secret door: 12 gems (50 gp each) + elven boots; secret door leads to Shrine storage (K-63)

---

**Lair K — Shrine of Evil Chaos**

Foul draft at entrance. Worn, echoing corridors (+2 chance of being surprised). Continual noise summons zombie guards in 3 rounds. Commanded by [[Wiki/NPCs/Shrine-Evil-Cleric]].

- **Room 51 — Boulder-Filled Passage:** 100 man-turns to clear; may lead outside SW or to Cave of the Unknown (DM's choice)
- **Room 52 — Hall of Skeletons:** Dais + gem throne (4 garnets 500 gp each); 12 skeletons — dormant until dais touched, then activate (amulets: turned as zombies)
- **Room 53 — Guard Room:** 8 zombies always present (amulets: turned as ghouls); no treasure
- **Room 54 — Acolytes' Chamber:** 4 acolytes (C1, chain mail under red robes); 10 gp each; leader has amulet of protection from good (+1 save, −1 opponent to-hit, blocks enchanted monster melee)
- **Room 55 — Chapel of Evil Chaos:** Mosaic floor, huge evil tapestry, stone altar with 4 ancient bronze vessels (bowl, 2 goblets, ewer — cursed relics; 1,000 gp/goblet, 2,000 gp each for others, but all are relics of evil; save vs. Magic at −2 or fall under demonic compulsion within 6 days, becoming a permanent shrine guard)
- **Room 56 — Adepts' Chamber:** 4 adepts (C2, plate under black robes); copper chains (40 gp each); 20 gp + 5 pp each; cause light wounds/cause fear/light spells; copper dishes on table (175 gp)
- **Room 57 — Hall of Undead Warriors:** 20 skeletons (chain mail rags, amulets: turned as zombies) + 20 zombies (chain mail, amulets: turned as ghouls); deploy to temple walls when great bell sounds
- **Room 58 — Temple of Evil Chaos:** Polished black stone floor; great iron bell; bone/ivory throne (10 black gems 100 gp each + 10 red gems 500 gp each + 1 large red gem 1,000 gp); black candles auto-light on entry; wall shapes mesmerize on failed save — 3+ chanting voices ring the bell automatically
- **Room 59g — Anteroom:** 3 plate-mailed zombies (AC 2, hp 13 each, turned as ghouls); lavish furnishings; golden flagon (500 gp) + 9 cups (100 gp each)
- **Room 59 — Evil Priest's Chamber:** [[Wiki/NPCs/Shrine-Evil-Cleric]]; escape via wardrobe → secret passage → corridor; 500 gp + 50 gems (10 gp each) spill from wardrobe as trap for pursuers
- **Room 60 — Guest Chamber:** Nothing of value; polished mirror under velvet cloth
- **Room 61 — Torture Chamber:** Torturer (F3, hp 19, battle axe, D 3–8); 135 gp + bracelet (700 gp) hidden in mattress
- **Room 62 — The Crypt:** Bolted door; many coffins and sarcophagi; sixth tomb holds Wight (HD 3★, drains 1 level); secret compartment in wight's tomb: sword +2, scroll of protection from undead, helm of alignment change, silver dagger with gems (800 gp)
- **Room 63 — Storage Chamber:** Gelatinous cube arrives after 3 rounds (AC 8, HD 4★, paralyzation); cube contains d12 each cp/sp/ep/gp/pp + wand of enemy detection (9 charges, ruined in 1 turn by digestive juices); secret door leads to Gnoll Room 50
- **Room 64 — Cell:** Iron door, locked and barred; medusa prisoner (AC 8, HD 4★★, hp 20; bite = asp hair = save vs. poison or die; looking at face = save or turn to stone); has potion of stone to flesh (6 uses) but will not give it willingly; captive medusa will promise to help if freed, then attempt to stone her rescuers

## GM Notes

*gm_only*

- Monster losses are permanent. The Caves deplete with each party incursion.
- Inter-lair alliances: Kobolds pay Ogre (250 gp in Room 18 barrel); Goblins flee east to Hobgoblins if losing; Orcs B and C share a secret meeting room (13); Gnolls and Orcs have a loose defensive pact. Only the Shrine (K) is truly coordinated.

**Faction non-coordination:** Factions do not automatically aid each other. Rival groups may prepare defensively when they hear nearby fighting but will not rush to assist enemies. Combat noise in one lair does not trigger automatic reinforcement from others — only specific inter-faction relationships produce that (see Gnoll-Orc pact, Goblin-Hobgoblin flight path, Bugbear-Minotaur mercenary chain).

**Morale failure behaviors are faction-specific — apply the appropriate one, not a generic "flee":**
- Goblins scatter in all directions
- Hobgoblins retreat in good order (repositioning, not breaking)
- Orcs fight desperately, push harder when cornered
- Bugbears break suddenly and violently when they do — but create dread through absence before that moment
- Ogre flees or fights to the death if cornered (no middle ground)

These distinctions matter for encounter feel and tactical consequences.
- Bugbear clan survives until all are dead except the chieftain, who flees to the Minotaur for help.
- The medusa in K-64 has a potion of stone to flesh (6 uses) — potentially powerful for a party that suffers stoning, but the medusa will not cooperate willingly.
- The four cursed bronze relics (K-55): dispel magic + bless removes the curse (60% on day 1, −10%/day through day 6).
- The great bell (K-58): sounding it deploys undead from Room 57 on the same round. Three chanting voices trigger it automatically.
- Room 51 boulder passage: 100 man-turns to clear; connects to outside or Cave of the Unknown (DM decides before play).
