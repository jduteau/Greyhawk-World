# Burne and Rufus's Tower

The guard tower (areas 30–31) in [[Wiki/Locations/Village-of-Hommlet]], home to [[Wiki/NPCs/Burne]] and [[Wiki/NPCs/Rufus]]. A 55' tower with a smaller inner turret rising at 35'. Adjacent to the beginnings of a new castle being built under Viscount and Archcleric funding (area 30 — early-stage earthworks and foundations).

## Overview

Entry is by a curving stone staircase terminating at a landing 10' above the ground; the outer door lowers to form a bridge. Arrow slits ring the structure; a splay thickens the base walls to 6'. Upper and lower battlements are machicolated with pierced merlons. Two men-at-arms watch from the roof.

Burne and Rufus came to Hommlet three years ago, adventured successfully (defeated a bandit force, reportedly killed a green dragon in the Kron Hills), and built this tower. Both are now funded by the Viscount and Archcleric in exchange for holding the region and reporting on untoward happenings.

## Geography

- **Room 1 — Main Hall (Ground Level):** Entry via lowered drawbridge (1" thick iron-bound oak, windlass-operated). Stone wall + iron inner door. Guard (F2, HP 12, long sword + shield) on duty; arrow slits + 2 murder holes flank the inner portal. Two fireplaces; cooking done by 2–3 servants. Judgment alcove with comfortable chairs; trestle tables for feasts of up to 40.
- **Room 2 — Lower Level (Ground, splay level):** Supplies — food, ale, beer, wine, oil. No windows or arrow slits.
- **Room 3 — Dungeon/Cellar Level:** Stabling for horses (Burne's light warhorse and Rufus's heavy warhorse normally kept here at night). **4 prison cells** (oaken door, barred, locked, and chained; fetters in each). Secret door leads to a small natural cave with a spring.
- **Room 4 — Hall and Ladderway:** Steep retractable wooden stairs (~3' wide, ladder-like) to upper levels. Guard (F2, HP 11, long sword + shield) on duty.
- **Room 5 — Rufus's Chamber:** Well-furnished. Longbow + quiver with 14 silver-tipped arrows and 6 × +2 arrows. Under a loose fireplace stone: iron box with 10 × 500 gp gems, 100 pp, 200 gp.
- **Room 6 — Burne's Chamber:** Spartan — a narrow pallet instead of a bed. Workbench, alembics, beakers, parchments, books. A large black staff leaning near the entry has Nystul's Magic Aura cast on it (not otherwise magical). Box under pallet: 3 daggers, 20 darts, dungeoneering equipment, 6 vials holy water, silver holy symbol, continual-light wand in ivory tube, silver knife. **Hidden flat chest amidst rafters** (looks like flooring): 51 × 1,000 gp gems, 17 × 500 gp gems, 100 × 10 gp gems. Locked iron coffer: 200 gp + ring with emerald (3,000 gp) + scroll of 7 spells (mirror image, pyrotechnics, web, read languages, suggestion, ice storm, rock to mud — Burne is debating whether to use them or save for emergency). Spell book disguised as the seat cushion of the only armchair.
- **Room 7 — Hall:** Ladderway from below exits here. Iron door to parapet (room 8). Ladderway continues to turret top. Barrel of 20 javelins beside the door.
- **Room 8 — Parapet:** Machicolated, pierced merlons. Two light catapults at marked positions with smooth boulders and small rocks stacked around them. Two guards on duty.
- **Room 9 — Mercenary Captain's Room:** Sparse. Score of arrows + 100 crossbow bolts near door. Small coffer: 173 pp + 21 × 1,000 gp gems. Captain: F5, HP 34, splint mail + +2 shield + +1 sword + crossbow + dagger (S 13, I 11, W 13, D 14, C 11, Ch 14).
- **Room 10 — Mercenary Lieutenant's Room:** Same layout; 8 quivers of arrows. Purse: 15 × 100 gp gems + 50 gp. Lieutenant: F4, HP 28, chain mail + +1 shield + flail + crossbow + dagger (S 16, I 9, W 10, D 12, C 15, Ch 10).
- **Room 11 — Top of Turret:** Racked pallets for the men-at-arms (sleep here when off duty).
- **Room 12 — Turret Roof:** Two scorpions (siege weapons), long box with 30 heavy spears, chest with rags and oil pots for flaming missiles.

**Mercenary garrison:** Captain (F5) + Lieutenant (F4) + 16 men-at-arms (mix of leather, ring, chain armor; various weapons; morale: high; loyalty: good — former brigands pardoned by Burne and Rufus). Some operate catapults and scorpions.

## GM Notes

*gm_only*

- Burne's spell book (disguised as armchair cushion) is the single most important item in the tower to protect. If it is found or taken, Burne's reaction will be severe.
- The hidden flat chest in the rafters requires someone to actively check the ceiling structure — it is not found by floor-level searching.
- The secret cave with spring (room 3) is an emergency water/escape route.
- Rufus reaches 8th level and returns to Verbobonc for special Viscount service — this is a long-term campaign clock.
- Both leaders and the mercenaries spend ample off-duty time at the [[Wiki/Locations/Welcome-Wench-Inn]]. This is a natural social connection point with the party.
