# The Keep on the Borderlands

A well-garrisoned frontier fortress serving as the primary home base for adventurers in [[Wiki/Modules/B2-Keep-on-the-Borderlands]]. Built of granite blocks with multiple defensive layers, it houses the Castellan, his garrison, merchants, clerics, and various travelers.

## Overview

The Keep is a lawful-aligned stronghold guarding the borderlands against the humanoid threats of the nearby [[Wiki/Locations/Caves-of-Chaos]]. It offers adventurers a safe place to rest, resupply, hire mercenaries, and receive services. The Castellan commands with bravery and honesty but can be hasty in decisions.

The Keep harbors a dangerous secret: the jovial Priest in the private apartments (area 7b) is actually a chaotic spy working to undermine expeditions to the Caves of Chaos.

## Geography

The Keep consists of an Outer Bailey and an Inner Bailey.

**Outer Bailey (areas 1–17):**
- **1. Main Gate** — drawbridge, portcullis, murder holes; two men-at-arms on guard
- **2. Flanking Towers** — crossbowmen plus longbow reinforcements; oil and rocks stored for defense
- **3. Entry Yard** — corporal of the watch (F2, sword +1, grouchy); scribe records all entry/exit; visitors must dismount
- **4. Common Stable** — 5–8 lackeys; 2–8 light horses, 2–8 draft horses, 1–4 mules
- **5. Common Warehouse** — chained/padlocked; corporal holds keys; two wagons of goods (~100 gp/wagon-load)
- **6. Bailiff's Tower** — Bailiff (F3, magic plate+1 or sword+1, hp 22); 12 men-at-arms; quiver with 20 arrows (3 magic +1) on wall; scribe's jewelled ink pot (100 gp, looks worthless, in plain sight)
- **7a. Private Apartments — Jewel Merchant** — normal man + wife, 2 F2 guards, 2 trained attack dogs; locked iron box (200 pp + 100 gp); 10 gems (10 gp each) in belt; wife wears bracelet/necklace/earrings (600/1,200/300 gp); buys gems at 60–90% of value
- **7b. Private Apartments — Evil Priest Spy** — see [[Wiki/NPCs/Evil-Priest-Spy]]
- **8. Smithy and Armorer** — Smith (F1, hp 11, hammer as weapon); 2 swords, 1 mace, chain mail, 11 spears in stock; 27 ep hidden in bedroom jar
- **9. Provisioner** — dungeon gear at rulebook prices; buys at 50%; 7 shields in stock; strong box (100 gp + 16 ep + 30 cp)
- **10. Trader** — all armor/weapons/bulk goods; buys furs at stated value; 500 gp + 1,110 sp under floorboards
- **11. Loan Bank** — 10% exchange fee; free storage for 1+ month deposits; loans up to 5 gp unsecured (10%/month interest); strong room vault in cellar (12 compartments with poison needle traps; compartment 3 = gold altar service 6,000 gp; compartment 6 = 1,000 each pp/gp/ep/sp/cp; compartment 5 = sleeping gas trap; compartment 7 = 4 pit vipers; compartment 8 = 3,000+ gp in gems)
- **12. Watch Tower (45')** — captain of the watch (F3, sword +2, dagger +1, hp 20); 12 men-at-arms; silver flagon + tankard (750 gp)
- **13. Fountain Square** — holiday market
- **14. Traveler's Inn** — 5 private rooms (1 gp/night), common room (1 sp/night); innkeeper family noncombatant
- **15. Tavern** — 4–16 patrons always present; 50% chance 2–5 mercenaries for hire (F1, 1 sp/day with gear or 1 gp/day without); 10% each: corporal/captain of watch, bailiff, evil priest, 2–4 watchmen, sergeant of guard, wanderer NPC
- **16. Guild House** — fee collection; guild master very influential and affects how garrison treats party; 4 fanatical men-at-arms guards; strong box (712 gp)
- **17. Chapel** — colored glass window (350 gp intact); offering box (1–100 cp + 1–100 sp, emptied nightly by Curate); [[Wiki/NPCs/The-Curate]] (C5) + 3 acolytes; secret compartment under offering pedestal (potions + scroll — both Curate's and Castellan's keys required)

**Inner Bailey (areas 18–27):**
- **18. Inner Gatehouse** — main armory; no visitors without invitation or permits; Captain of the Guard (F3, AC 0, sword+2, spear+1, 15 gp + 150 gp gem in dagger pommel); Sergeant of Guard (F2, Str 17, sword+1, dagger+1, potion of healing in room); 24 guardsmen
- **19. Small Tower** — 8 guardsmen (chain mail, crossbows/swords)
- **20. Guard Tower (50')** — 24 guardsmen; corporal of the guard (sword, dagger +1)
- **21. Inner Bailey** — grass-covered drill and practice ground; soldiers training during daylight
- **22. Cavalry Stables** — 30 war horses, 1–4 riding horses; 2 lackeys, 2 men-at-arms on guard
- **23. Great Tower (60')** — 24 guardsmen; corporal
- **24. Keep Fortress** — great hall, cavalry armory, meeting chambers; cellars with vast stores, cistern, dungeon (4 cells); Castellan holds audience here daily; 8 plate-armored crossbowmen on wall at all times + 8 inside
- **25. Corner Towers (40')** — men-at-arms quarters
- **26. Central Towers** — Castellan's assistants: Scribe (C2, plate+shield, hold person scroll, light spell, gold holy symbol 150 gp); Advisor (E3, Dex 16, plate+1, ring of fire resistance, short bow at +2, arrows+1, spells charm/read magic/web, jeweled pendant 1,000 gp)
- **27. Castellan's Chamber** — private room of [[Wiki/NPCs/The-Castellan]]; silver mirror (300 gp), malachite bowl (750 gp), fox robe (1,200 gp), silver case (450 gp) with 40 pp + 12 gems (100 gp each); spear +1 by door

## GM Notes

*gm_only*

- The spy priest (7b) is chaotic evil; he will join parties heading to the Caves and betray them at a critical encounter. His two acolytes never speak (cover story: vows of silence). All three are evil.
- The Curate distrusts the Priest (50% chance to reveal if asked by a trusted friend). Curate's own acolytes like the priest — contradiction the party can investigate.
- Entry scribe (area 3) records all comings and goings — the spy priest can track party movements through this record.
- Castellan's two assistants (area 26) are more dangerous than they appear: Scribe is a C2 cleric; Advisor is an E3 elf with charm and web spells.
- The Banker's vault (area 11) compartment 3 holds a gold altar service worth 6,000 gp — one of the biggest non-dungeon treasures in the module.
- Captain of the Guard (area 18) sometimes disguises as a mercenary in the Outer Bailey — useful for secret aid to the party.
