# Session 2 — The Caves and the Kobold Parley

## Summary

The party visited the Chapel and Castellan Sherlane, receiving two healing potions and a writ of authority before venturing to the Caves of Chaos. They successfully negotiated with the kobold tribe, learning that the cave tribes are being commanded by an unknown force, prisoners are being taken alive to the deep caves, and something old dwells in the large northern cave. Conrad fell into a pit trap on exit and was critically wounded, forcing a retreat. Back at the Keep, the party hired two men-at-arms—Eckhardt and Marten—then returned to the Caves at dawn, ready to assault the goblin lair.

## Narrative

The morning sun broke cold and pale over the Keep on the Borderlands as the party gathered in the inner bailey. Before venturing to the Caves of Chaos, Thomas suggested visiting the Chapel to speak with the Curate about healing supplies and information.

At the Chapel, the Curate—a middle-aged man carrying a curious staff carved with a serpent's head—received them with measured interest. When Thomas spoke of their suspicions about coordinated evil in the Caves and the reoccupied Moat House near Hommlet, the Curate's demeanor shifted to grave concern. He spoke quietly of the Temple of Elemental Evil, broken a generation ago but perhaps stirring once more. He also warned them to be wary of another priest dwelling in the outer bailey, whose presence troubled his spirit.

The Curate brought them to Castellan Sherlane, advocating for the party's cause. Though Sherlane remained cautious—demanding proof before committing the Keep's resources—he authorized the release of two healing potions and provided a sealed writ identifying them as acting with his knowledge. He charged them to bring back concrete evidence: symbols, documents, or prisoners who could speak of dark masters.

The party traveled four miles east through increasingly wild country until they reached the Caves of Chaos—a great ravine honeycombed with cave mouths. Lance scouted ahead, identifying orc lairs on the west wall, a fortified hobgoblin cave with a barred door, kobold and goblin caves on the east wall, a suspiciously welcoming cave with multilingual signs, and a large cave at the ravine's northern terminus from which cold air emanated.

Choosing to start with the kobolds as the easiest target, the party descended into the ravine and approached the small cave mouth on the eastern wall. Inside, they were challenged by kobold warriors, but Thomas's decision to parley rather than fight led to an unexpected audience with Chief Ishka and his shaman.

Though the Chief was hostile and suspicious, the shaman—fearful of something worse than the adventurers—eventually revealed crucial intelligence: the tribes were being commanded by someone or something, working together despite ancient enmities. Prisoners were being taken alive into the deep caves, not for food or ransom but for unknown purposes. The kobolds heard chanting at night and felt a wrongness emanating from the large cave at the ravine's back. Something old dwelt there, and even the rats fled from it.

As the party departed with this intelligence, Conrad triggered a concealed pit trap near the cave entrance, plunging ten feet onto sharpened stakes. The wound was grievous—he fell unconscious at death's door. Thomas used his laying on hands to stabilize him, and the party retreated to the Keep with their wounded companion.

At the Chapel, the Curate tended to Conrad, confirming he would need a week's rest before any strenuous activity. The party then reported to Castellan Sherlane, who received their intelligence with grim satisfaction. The news of coordinated tribes and prisoners taken alive was proof enough that something dangerous was growing in those caves. He provided fifty gold pieces for supplies and hiring men-at-arms, urging them to discover who commanded the tribes and what happened to the prisoners before the darkness grew strong enough to emerge.

The party sought the corporal of the watch, who directed them to two recently mustered-out soldiers: Eckhardt, a steady spearman, and Marten, an ambitious young swordsman. Both were hired at the standard rate of one gold piece per day plus a half-share of treasure. A hooded woman in the tavern rebuffed Thomas's recruitment attempt with cold suspicion.

After securing their new hirelings and checking on Conrad one final time, the party rested at the Traveler's Inn. They departed at first light the next morning—now six strong—and returned to the Caves of Chaos. As they stood at the ravine's rim, studying the goblin cave higher on the eastern wall, Marten asked the question on everyone's mind: which cave would they assault first?
