# Dual Temple Connection

**Campaign hypothesis.** The source texts treat these as separate organizations. This page argues the case — from structural evidence within the wiki — that the [[Wiki/Factions/Temple-of-Evil-Chaos]] (B2) is a field operation subordinate to, or allied with, the [[Wiki/Factions/Temple-of-Elemental-Evil]] (T1/T2). Nothing in canon confirms this. Everything below is inference available to the GM.

## The Case

### 1. Identical Operational Doctrine

Both temples deploy the same infiltration playbook independently, in different locations:

| Element | Temple of Evil Chaos (B2) | Temple of Elemental Evil (T1) |
|---------|--------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Cover identity | Jovial visiting theologian (area 7b) | Waiting-for-caravan merchant (Welcome Wench room 1) |
| Asset type | [[Wiki/NPCs/Evil-Priest-Spy]] — cleric, C-level | Zert — fighter, low-level |
| Social engineering | Built universal goodwill; Keep speaks well of him | Drinks heavily, seems harmless |
| Betrayal method | Joins party; causes light wounds / blinds mid-combat | Joins party; attacks after successful run |
| Handler structure | Reports to [[Wiki/NPCs/Shrine-Evil-Cleric]] (K-59) | Reports to Rannos Davi → brigand courier → Temple hierarchy |

Two CE religious factions independently developing identical doctrine is possible. It is more parsimonious that the doctrine was shared — that these agents were trained or briefed by the same higher authority.

### 2. The Shrine Cleric's Unspecified Escape

[[Wiki/NPCs/Shrine-Evil-Cleric]] carries a potion of gaseous form specifically to flee via the K-51 boulder passage, which leads outside the Caves of Chaos to an unspecified exterior. The module names no destination. He also drops 500 gp + 50 gems as a pursuit trap — suggesting he is protecting someone else's investment in his survival, not just his own.

Where does he go? The most useful GM answer: he follows a pre-arranged escape route to [[Wiki/Factions/Temple-of-Elemental-Evil]] territory. This explains both the contingency planning and the silence about the destination — it was classified even in the module design.

### 3. Temporal Fit: Rebuilding After Defeat

The ToEE was "recently" militarily defeated by the Viscount of Verbobonc and the Archcleric of Veluna. Its response was to go underground — embedding a spy network in [[Wiki/Locations/Village-of-Hommlet]] while the main temple remains dormant. 

A CE organization with surviving leadership, seeking to rebuild covertly, would naturally expand its forward operations. The Caves of Chaos region represents exactly the kind of frontier territory where a subordinate shrine could operate with plausible deniability — far enough from Verbobonc that the Viscount's agents (Elmo, Jaroo, Burne) aren't watching.

### 4. Structural Parallel: Corruption Architecture

Both temples use the same ultimate conversion mechanism:

- **B2 Shrine (K-55):** Four cursed bronze relics — save vs. Magic at −2 or become a permanent servant of Chaos within 6 days. The character physically returns to the Shrine as a guard.
- **ToEE:** The Temple's broader operations (T2) are built around elemental corruption and permanent allegiance conversion.

Permanent conversion of good-aligned adventurers into temple servants is a highly specific doctrine. Finding it in both organizations, independently, is the strongest doctrinal parallel available.

### 5. The TZGY Scarab as Access Protocol

The TZGY scarab found in the traders' locked coffer (area 13, [[Wiki/Locations/Village-of-Hommlet]]) is a Temple of Elemental Evil pass — a physical authentication token. The Shrine Evil Cleric, if connected to the ToEE network, would carry or know the equivalent. This pass represents a communication layer between field operations: agents who need to move between temples or seek sanctuary carry them.

If the Shrine Cleric's escape route leads to ToEE territory, a TZGY scarab (or equivalent) is what gets him through the door.

## The Hypothesis

> The Temple of Evil Chaos is a regional forward operation of the Temple of Elemental Evil — established before the ToEE's military defeat, now operating semi-autonomously. The Shrine Cleric reports up through a chain of command that eventually reaches ToEE leadership. The shared operational doctrine (spy-then-betray, cursed conversion relics, hierarchical compartmentalization) was standardized at the ToEE level and deployed in both locations.
>
> The Caves of Chaos serve the ToEE's long-term rebuilding strategy: a frontier foothold generating chaos and interdicting the adventuring parties most likely to threaten the Temple proper.

## How This Could Surface in Play

*gm_only*

- The Shrine Evil Cleric, if captured rather than killed, knows the escape route and destination. Interrogation reveals it.
- A TZGY scarab (or equivalent marking) could be found on the spy priest or in the Shrine's hidden correspondence — matching the one in Rannos Davi's coffer.
- The brigand courier that carries messages from Hommlet to the ToEE hierarchy could be ambushed; captured letters mention "our Borderlands shrine" or similar.
- A sage who identifies the TZGY scarab (20% chance) might also recognize the Shrine's amulets or bronze relics as related iconography.
- If the party moves from B2 to T1 without knowing they're connected, Elmo or Jaroo might recognize a relic or symbol from the Caves as Temple-affiliated — triggering the connection from the NPC side.

## Evidence Gaps

These parallels are structural, not documentary. No physical letter, sigil, or NPC statement in either module explicitly links the two organizations. The connection requires the GM to treat the two modules as taking place in a shared regional setting where one organization plausibly operates at multiple sites — which Greyhawk geography supports but does not mandate.

## Status

Unconfirmed. No in-game evidence has surfaced. Campaign has not begun.

## Larger Context

*gm_only*

The Dual Temple Connection is itself a component of a deeper structure. See [[Wiki/Conspiracy/Lolth-Overarching-Conspiracy]] — both the Temple of Evil Chaos (B2) and the Temple of Elemental Evil (T1) are ultimately layers of Lolth's multi-front campaign. The structural parallels identified in this page (identical infiltration doctrine, cursed conversion relics, compartmentalized hierarchy) reflect shared training and direction from the same ultimate authority.

The Dual Temple hypothesis argues these temples coordinate with each other. The overarching conspiracy explains why: they were both built from the same blueprint, by the same ultimate power.
