# Lolth's Overarching Conspiracy

Lolth, the Spider Queen of the drow, has been running a multi-front destabilization campaign against the surface world for decades. Her agents work in layers, each knowing only as much as they need to. The party has been fighting this conspiracy since the day they entered the Caves of Chaos — though they will not know it until deep in the D-series.

*Never reveal this directly. Let the players discover it piece by piece.*

## DROW TIMELINE — HARD RULE

**Drow must not appear physically in any form until G3 Hall of the Fire Giant King.**

Before that point: no drow NPCs, no drow artifacts connected to the conspiracy, no direct drow communication the party can witness. Drow presence is limited strictly to the breadcrumb items in the schedule — indirect traces only. The mystery must precede the revelation by many sessions.

## The Eight Layers

| Layer | Module | Organization | Knowledge Level |
|-------|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | T1 / B2 | Lareth, Hommlet cult network, Caves humanoids | Knows handlers; doesn't understand the wider network |

### Layer 1 — The Hommlet Cult Network (T1 / B2)

[[Wiki/NPCs/Lareth-the-Beautiful]] coordinates the humanoid activity at the [[Wiki/Locations/Caves-of-Chaos]] and the cult agents inside [[Wiki/Locations/Village-of-Hommlet]] ([[Wiki/NPCs/Rannos-Davi]], [[Wiki/NPCs/Gremag]]). He is the party's first direct encounter with the conspiracy's infrastructure — capable and dangerous, but reporting upward through channels he doesn't fully understand. Evidence in the Moathouse connects the Caves, the village, and a network pointing south and east.

### Layer 2 — The Shrine of Evil Chaos (B2)

A local cult node feeding into Lareth's operation. Cultists know only their immediate handlers. The [[Wiki/NPCs/Shrine-Evil-Cleric]] wears a silver spider amulet of drow workmanship — cold to the touch regardless of temperature, slightly heavier than it should be. He received it from a coastal merchant contact. He does not know its origin.

### Layer 3 — Darkshelf Quarry (A0 — optional bridge)

A slaver staging post at a coastal quarry (characters ~level 3–4). Optional but useful as a bridge level or if the party follows the coastal merchant lead from the B2 breadcrumb. Plants direct evidence of slaver operations: branded prisoners, coded manifests, a Brotherhood signet. Not required but creates a natural on-ramp to A1.

### Layer 4 — The Brotherhood of the Slave Lords (A1-4)

[[Wiki/Factions/Brotherhood-of-Slave-Lords]] is sophisticated but receives direction from a drow intermediary: [[Wiki/NPCs/Edralve]]. The slavers believe she is a well-connected fence. She is not.

### Layer 5 — WG4 Forgotten Temple of Tharizdun (detour)

Not part of the Lolth conspiracy directly, but a deliberate detour between the A-series and G-series. The temple has been sealed for centuries. This encounter recontextualises Lolth: Tharizdun is vastly more ancient evil; Lolth actively avoids him, which makes her more formidable — she is a power even the Elder Eye cannot command.

> "Even the lords of the deep places bow before the Elder Eye — all save the Spider Queen, who serves nothing but her own hunger." *(Temple inscription)*

### Layer 6 — The Giants (G1-3)

[[Wiki/NPCs/Eclavdra]] of House Eilservs supplies giant chiefs with enchanted weapons and promises of surface territory. She is physically present in G3 and escapes when the party arrives. This is the first moment drow appear in person.

### Layer 7 — The Underdark (D1-3)

The party follows Eclavdra's trail into the Underdark. D3 (Vault of the Drow) is a functioning drow city. Eclavdra's operation was a House Eilservs initiative — not sanctioned by all drow. Some houses are horrified that it has attracted surface attention. Lolth herself has said nothing, which means she is pleased.

The D3 temple mural depicts a spider goddess receiving tribute from giants, slavers, surface cultists, and humanoid warbands — all tiny figures. This is not propaganda. It is accurate history. The entire campaign, in one image.

### Layer 8 — The Demonweb (Q1)

Lolth's home plane. The conspiracy ends here.

## Breadcrumb Schedule

**BREADCRUMB LIMIT**: At most one meaningful conspiracy clue per session, unless the party is actively investigating a lead or a major milestone location is reached.

Clues are indirect traces — objects, fragments, half-remembered descriptions. Never physical drow presence before G3. Not all will be found — that's fine. Multiple hints point to the same truth so no single clue is load-bearing.

### T1 / B2 Phase (Levels 1–3)

- **Drow coin — Lareth's strongbox** (T1 Moathouse): A flat black disc stamped with a spider. Markings in unknown script. Michael's Read Magic deciphers them as a maker's mark — elegant and alien. A sage in Hommlet can identify it as coming from "deep below" but knows nothing more.
- **Spider amulet — Shrine priest** (B2 Caves): A silver amulet of unusual workmanship — spider in dark metal, tiny inscriptions around the rim. Cold to the touch always. Slightly heavier than it should be. Michael's Read Magic deciphers the inscription: a name or title in the same angular script as the coin. It was a gift. The priest doesn't know from whom.
- **Rescued prisoner testimony** (B2 Caves): A prisoner mentions being "inspected" by a tall, dark-skinned figure with white hair. She thought it was a dream — she'd been drugged. She can describe the figure's eyes as "red, like coals."

*The coin and amulet inscription are in the same script — reward Michael's Read Magic with this recognition.*

### A1-4 Phase (Levels 5–7)

- **Dockmaster's ledger** (A1 Highport): Regular shipments to a buyer identified only as "E." Destination encodes toward the Pomarj coast and then down. "The lower road" appears twice.
- **Edralve's traces**: Drow-crafted manacles unlike anything surface smiths make. A burned letter fragment referencing "the Great Work above" and "the lords of the deep places."
- **Senior Slavelord's hidden shrine** (A4): A small spider-motif shrine. He is a secret Lolth worshipper who received his faith through dreams — never met a drow. The shrine has the same cold, heavy quality as the amulet from the Caves. The party may recognize it.
- **Captured Slavelord testimony**: "A woman — not human, I think — who came twice a year. She wore a hood always. She smelled of deep stone and she never ate. She paid in magic."

### WG4 Phase (Levels 7–9)

- **Temple inscription — intact section**: *"Even the lords of the deep places bow before the Elder Eye — all save the Spider Queen, who serves nothing but her own hunger."*
- **Temple inscription — partially legible**: *"She was offered dominion. She refused. She cannot be offered anything. She takes."* No name — the party can infer.
- **Aldric's moment**: St. Cuthbert's faith is ordered and rational; what he senses here is neither. He will not talk about it afterward. Play this as a character beat, not a mechanical event.

### G1-3 Phase (Levels 9–11)

- **G1 — Hill giant's hand crossbow**: Drow construction; a gift from their patron. The giant chief calls the patron "the dark lady."
- **G2 — Caged drow messenger**: A frost giant jarl keeps a drow messenger as a curiosity. If freed, the messenger will not be grateful — but out of contempt for the giants will tell the party one true thing: *"The giants are tools. Tools get discarded when the work is done."*
- **G3 — Eclavdra's departure**: She is present, coldly beautiful, utterly unconcerned until she realizes the party can end the operation. She leaves with contemptuous efficiency, collapsing the entrance behind her. A hobgoblin mercenary saw her go through a hidden door — he'll say where, for the right price.

### D1-3 Phase (Levels 11–13)

- **D1-2 — Surface-world slaves**: One was captured in a raid on a village the Ranger may have used as a backstory location. Personal. Deliberate.
- **D3 — Temple mural**: Spider goddess receiving tribute from giants, slavers, surface cultists, humanoid warbands — all tiny figures. The campaign, in one image.
- **D3 — Political context**: Eclavdra's operation was not sanctioned by all drow. House Eilservs is a minority faction. Lolth herself has said nothing, which means she is pleased.
