# T1/B2 Cross-Module Thread

The operations of [[Wiki/Factions/Temple-of-Elemental-Evil]] run simultaneously through two fronts: the humanoid Caves of Chaos (B2 / [[Wiki/Locations/Caves-of-Chaos]]) and the Moat House dungeon operation (T1 / [[Wiki/Locations/Moat-House]]). These fronts are not independent — they share a command chain, supply lines, and communication infrastructure. The party is currently unaware of any connection between them.

**Distinct from:** [[Wiki/Conspiracy/Temple-Spy-Thread]] (the B2 Caves spy ring at the Keep) and [[Wiki/Conspiracy/ToEE-Spy-Network-Hommlet]] (the T1 Hommlet agent network). Those are component threads. This page tracks the cross-module linkage.

## The Thread

[[Wiki/NPCs/Lareth-the-Beautiful]] — known to his agents only as "the Shepherd" — commands the overall operation from the [[Wiki/Locations/Moat-House]] dungeon level. The Caves humanoid network is a forward operation that raids, takes prisoners, and clears the road of witnesses; the Hommlet trader network ([[Wiki/NPCs/Rannos-Davi]], [[Wiki/NPCs/Gremag]]) supplies both fronts and passes intelligence.

The [[Wiki/NPCs/Evil-Priest-Spy]] in the Keep receives monthly instructions from Lareth via goblin courier. The Shrine Evil Cleric similarly reports to Lareth by courier.

## Known Evidence

## Hidden Evidence

*gm_only*

### Physical Evidence — Caves of Chaos

- **Coded note on the Shrine priest** (body or Shrine chamber) — a written communication using merchant symbols from a contact in Hommlet. References supply routes and "the shepherd to the south." Conrad's thief skills or careful searching can find it.
- **Crates with trading post mark** (Shrine stores) — multiple crates bearing a mark matching the Rannos/Gremag traders' establishment in Hommlet. Supplies are flowing from the village to the Caves.
- **Prisoner testimony** — a prisoner held in the deeper caves was captured on the road. Before being taken she noticed her captors were met by a well-dressed man from the direction of Hommlet who handed over a purse and left quickly.

### Physical Evidence — Moat House

- **Lareth's strongbox document** — lists humanoid clan strengths in the Caves (troop numbers, cave assignments, current dispositions). He is running the operation from the Moat House end. Follow module text for strongbox access.
- **Note fragment in Lareth's effects** — references "our friends in the village" and asks for a count of travellers using the Keep road. This is Rannos and Gremag's reporting function made explicit.

### Trail Evidence — Goblinoid Roads

The trails south of the Keep road are active logistics routes — supply runs and prisoner transfers, not raiding parties. A skilled tracker can read:

- Multiple sets of tracks, not a single group: regular traffic, not a raid
- Old and fresh tracks mixed: this route has been used for months
- Drag marks: prisoners or heavy loads
- The trail leads toward the Moat House direction but diverges before the main road — whoever uses it knows the territory

### Communication Chain

```
Lareth ("the Shepherd") — Moat House
    ↓  monthly instructions via goblin courier
Evil Priest Spy — Keep
    ↓  monthly report via goblin courier

Lareth ("the Shepherd") — Moat House
    ↑  supply runs + intelligence
Rannos / Gremag — Hommlet traders
    (know Lareth only by code name; never met in person; dead drops on road)

Lareth ("the Shepherd") — Moat House
    ↑  intelligence + prisoner deliveries
Shrine Evil Cleric — Caves K (via goblin courier monthly)
```

## Timing Consequences

*gm_only*

Party actions in the Caves cascade to the Moat House:

- **Shrine priest killed or captured:** Monthly report to Lareth stops. After two missed reports, Lareth sends a goblin scout. If the scout doesn't return, Lareth knows the Caves operation is compromised and begins preparing to abandon the Moat House.
- **Rannos or Gremag learn party is investigating Caves seriously:** They send warning via dead drop. Lareth receives this within two days and raises alert status.
- **Party recovers the trading post mark and confronts Rannos:** He denies it but becomes visibly frightened and sends an urgent warning to Lareth that night.

These consequences make expedition speed and information security meaningful: a party that moves quickly and doesn't telegraph their Caves discoveries will find Lareth unprepared. A party that telegraphs their progress may find the Moat House on alert — or abandoned.
