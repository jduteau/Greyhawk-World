# Nira Melubb

Proprietor of the moneychanger's shop (area 15) in [[Wiki/Locations/Village-of-Hommlet]]. 3rd level thief (non-active). The sign shows three yellow balls.

**Stats:** S 9, I 16, W 13, D 15, C 9, Ch 10 · HP 12 · +1 dagger (boot), +1 sword (under counter).

## Description

Always obliging, polite, and businesslike. Asks no questions. Has a guard near the door (3rd level fighter, HP 17, chain mail, crossbow and sword) and two war dogs (2+2 HD, HP 16/13, 2-8 hp bite each) always nearby.

## Services

- **Coin exchange:** Any coins or metal for other exchange media; 10% fee on value.
- **Gems and jewelry:** Buys at 50–80% of actual value; sells at 102–120% above actual; fabricates on commission (cost = materials value × 2, which becomes the new item's value).
- **Restocking:** Can obtain new gem/jewelry stock every 7–12 days from passing merchants at market value. Track transactions — once supply is exhausted, it stays exhausted until resupply.

## Role

Professional moneychanger and gem dealer. The only financial services provider in Hommlet for coin exchange and jewelry transactions. Not affiliated with any faction; not interested in politics or allegiance. His only concern is that the village remains stable enough for business to continue — which makes the Temple's resurgence his enemy in purely economic terms.

## Personality & Voice

- Business-first, always: greetings are brief, the transaction is the point; he does not waste time on small talk but is never rude
- Asks no questions about the source of coins or gems — this is his professional signature and he means it; he extends the same courtesy he expects in return
- Quiet and precise in speech; names prices exactly and does not haggle; the posted rates are the rates
- Watchful without being threatening; the guard by the door and the war dogs are simply part of the shop's decor as far as he's concerned

## Goals

- Run a profitable, stable business; every transaction logged, every debt settled
- Protect his accumulated wealth; the tiered security of his shop is a deliberate architecture, not paranoia
- Maintain neutrality with all factions — he has no interest in being drawn into village politics or Temple conflicts
- Ensure Hommlet remains a safe and functional market town; instability is bad for a moneychanger's trade

## Knowledge

- Knows the current exchange rates and gem values for the region; can appraise jewelry and gems with accuracy
- Has handled coin from distant regions and unusual mintings; if shown the drow coin from Lareth's strongbox, he can confirm it is not surface currency and that the metal is unusual — but knows nothing more
- Tracks passing merchants who supply his gem stock; may have useful commercial intelligence about trade routes and who has been moving through the area
- Can confirm that prices at the traders (Rannos Davi) are inflated; professional knowledge, not allegiance
