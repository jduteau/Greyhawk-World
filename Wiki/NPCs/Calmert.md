# Calmert

Assistant cleric at the Church of St. Cuthbert in [[Wiki/Locations/Village-of-Hommlet]] (area 20). 3rd level cleric. He is the first person a party encounters when visiting the church.

**Stats:** S 8, I 10, W 16, D 15, C 9, Ch 11 · HP 14 · Plate mail + shield, +4 mace.

**Typical spells:** Bless, Command, Detect Evil, Detect Magic (1st) · Chant, Know Alignment, Silence 15'r (2nd)

## Description

Young, eager, and conspicuously pious — or appearing so. Invites visitors to sit on hard benches (not the padded chairs near the fireplace), directs them to religious tracts, prominently indicates the poorbox (emptied after each visitor), and steps out for 2d12 minutes before returning to discuss their business.

He is known throughout the village for his zeal in obtaining contributions.

## Role

- Day-to-day face of the church for all visitors
- Gatekeepers access to [[Wiki/NPCs/Terjon]]
- Operates the bell tower chimes (dawn, noon, dusk, Godsday services) — this is a demanding technical duty requiring specific melodies and patterns
- Collects contributions and handles routine pastoral care
- Quarters in area 13 of the church upper floor; one of his large black bell-rope beads is actually a 10 gp lump of platinum — his entire personal cache

## GM Truth

*gm_only*

- **The honest scam:** Calmert IS a genuinely honest man. But most of the miscellaneous money he collects for "the church" from adventurers and visitors goes toward funding the castle construction by [[Wiki/NPCs/Burne]] and [[Wiki/NPCs/Rufus]]. He considers this entirely appropriate. This is not embezzlement — it's good works for the village fortification.
- Visitors seeking the chief cleric ([[Wiki/NPCs/Terjon]]) must first pay Calmert (1–10 gp per level of the strongest character), then face a 50% chance Terjon is unavailable and they must return later (same routine).
- Exception: the injured, sick, and genuinely needy are not put through the routine — though all aid comes with an expectation of return according to ability.
