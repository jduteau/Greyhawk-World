# Brother Smyth

Village smith of [[Wiki/Locations/Village-of-Hommlet]] (area 8). 3rd level druid. Short, brawny, obviously hardworking and forthright. His forge and bellows identify his trade — a horseshoe nailed on three faces of the post marks the smithy.

**Stats:** S 17, I 9, W 13, D 10, C 15, Ch 15 · HP 19 · Leather armor, quarterstaff, hammer.

## Description

Likable and straightforward. He will openly discuss his work and religion with visitors. His druidical calling is no secret to the village.

## Services

- All general metalwork
- Weapon heads, caps, and shield work in iron and steel
- **Not** capable of fine armoring (will readily admit this)

## Role

Assistant to [[Wiki/NPCs/Jaroo-Ashstaff]], the Druid of the Grove. When Jaroo is absent, Brother Smyth steps in as religious advisor to the druidical faithful of Hommlet. He will never advance very high in the druidical hierarchy because his smith's occupation is incompatible with higher druidical ranks — he knows this and has accepted it as the price of his calling.

He reports unusual events to Jaroo (as does the woodcutter at area 3 and others in the druidical community).

In no event will he leave his forge to go adventuring.

## GM Notes

*gm_only*

- Wealth: small chest with 28 sp and 82 cp. He gives the remainder of his income to charitable work for the village needy.
- A party that treats the druidical community respectfully (making offerings at the Grove, not threatening Smyth's forge) gains a passive intelligence network — the druidical residents of Hommlet notice and report unusual activity.
- Smyth's high Strength (17) means he is a capable fighter if the village is threatened; his quarterstaff should not be underestimated.
