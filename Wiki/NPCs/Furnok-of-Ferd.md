# Furnok of Ferd

Occupant of upper room 8 at the [[Wiki/Locations/Welcome-Wench-Inn]]. 4th level thief. A professional gambler who makes his living by cheating passing merchants at dice and cards.

**Stats:** S 8, I 14, W 10, D 18, C 15, Ch 13 · HP 18 · Leather armor, ring of invisibility, scroll of protection from magic, short sword, +1 dagger (hidden in boot).

## Description

Confident, personable, and careful. He plays dice and cards daily in the common room, running a modest but consistent winning streak using loaded knucklebones and nicked cards — but never winning enough to be caught. He sizes up marks quickly: merchants are his preference; adventurers are a riskier but more lucrative prospect.

**Gambling probabilities:** 75% chance of winning at dice; 60% at cards. He manages these odds carefully to avoid suspicion.

## Role

Self-employed thief and gambler. No faction agenda, no quest. He is at the inn because there is a steady stream of merchants and travelers to fleece.

## Party Relationship

Not yet met. Furnok will offer to join an adventuring party for **equal share** of treasure, plus whatever he can quietly lift — especially magic items, which he covets. He will offer his **scroll of protection from magic** as a good-faith deposit to seal the deal. This is not a trick — the scroll is genuinely valuable and he considers it a real investment.

The hidden catch: Furnok will pilfer from the party throughout the expedition. He will not betray them, abandon them in a fight, or cause their deaths — but anything portable and valuable that he can take without being caught, he will take.

## What the Party Knows

Not yet revealed.

## GM Notes

*gm_only*

- Furnok is not evil. He is a thief by trade, not inclination toward violence. He will not participate in or allow murder of party members.
- His ring of invisibility is his emergency exit. If the situation turns very bad, he will use it to escape.
- The equal-share deal is genuine on its face. He will pull his weight in non-combat roles (scouting, lock-picking, trap detection). His 4th-level thief skills are real.
- He has a small hoard of gems in his room: 37 × 50 gp gems; 12 sp and 12 gp for gambling stakes.
